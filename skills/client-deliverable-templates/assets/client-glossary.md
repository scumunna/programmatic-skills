# Client glossary

The plain-language version of the terms a client hears in a report or a meeting. Use it to
swap jargon for a sentence the client understands, or paste the relevant rows into a deck
appendix. Each term gets one plain sentence, no math and no internal detail.

This is the client-facing counterpart to the practitioner glossary in the
`programmatic-foundations` skill. When you need the precise definition, the formula, or the
"why it matters" for your own use, read that one. When you are writing for the client, use the
sentence here.

| Term | What it means, in one sentence for the client |
| --- | --- |
| Viewability | Whether an ad actually had the chance to be seen on screen, rather than loading somewhere the person never scrolled to. |
| Win rate | How often we won the chance to show your ad out of the times we tried to buy it. |
| CPA (cost per acquisition) | The average amount of media spend it took to get one result, like a sale or a sign-up. |
| CPM (cost per thousand) | The price to show your ad one thousand times, which is how display and video media is usually priced. |
| CTR (click-through rate) | The share of people who saw your ad and then clicked it. |
| ROAS (return on ad spend) | How much revenue came back for every dollar of media spend, so a 4x ROAS means four dollars of revenue per dollar spent. |
| Impressions | The number of times your ad was shown, counting every single display. |
| Frequency | The average number of times the same person saw your ad over the campaign. |
| Programmatic | Buying ad space automatically and in real time, deciding for each individual ad slot whether it is worth showing your ad there. |
| DSP (demand-side platform) | The software we use to buy your ads across many sites and apps from one place. |
| Attribution | How we decide which ads to credit when someone buys, since most people see several touches before they act. |
| Incrementality | The extra results your ads actually caused, beyond the sales that would have happened anyway without them. |
| Conversion | A result that counts as success for your goal, such as a purchase, a lead, or a sign-up. |

When a client asks what one of these means, give them the sentence from the right-hand
column, not the technical definition. If a term you need is not here, write one plain
sentence in the same style and add it, and check the practitioner glossary in
`programmatic-foundations` so your sentence stays accurate.
